Outdoor Exercises

Whether you're bored with no plans, a student on a budget, or just taking a break from revision, here are some easy exercises to do outside to get some fresh air!

Brisk Walking

Explore your surroundings by taking a brisk walk or power walk in your neighborhood or local parks. It's a great low-impact exercise that improves cardiovascular health.

Jogging

Hit the streets or local trails for a jog or run. Running is an excellent aerobic exercise that helps burn calories and boosts endurance.

Biking

Take your bike out for a ride and enjoy the fresh air while improving your cardiovascular fitness. Explore bike paths or cycle-friendly routes in your area.

Hiking

Find nearby hiking trails and embark on nature walks. Hiking not only provides a good workout but also allows you to connect with nature and enjoy beautiful scenery.

Yoga

Practice yoga or Pilates in the open air. Find a quiet spot in a park or your backyard, and follow online videos or attend outdoor group classes if available.

Interval Training

Create a circuit of exercises like jumping jacks, push-ups, squats, and lunges. Perform each exercise for a set amount of time, alternating between high-intensity bursts and short rest periods.

Stair Climbing

Look for outdoor staircases or bleachers and challenge yourself with stair climbing workouts. This activity is great for strengthening leg muscles and improving cardiovascular fitness.

Sports

Engage in team sports like basketball, soccer, or volleyball, or try individual sports like tennis or golf. Many parks have courts or fields available for public use.

Kayaking

If you have access to a nearby lake, river, or coastline, try kayaking or canoeing. These activities provide a full-body workout while enjoying the water and surrounding scenery.
